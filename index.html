Military-grade encryption that happens entirely in your browser. No servers. No databases. No data collection. Just math you can verify.

Client-side encryption

Your message is encrypted using AES-GCM with 256-bit keys before it ever leaves your device. The encryption happens in JavaScript, right in your browser. No backend. No API calls. No trust needed.

No servers involved

The encrypted content lives in the URL hash (#). Browsers never send the hash fragment to servers—it's part of the web standard. Open your Network tab and watch: zero requests. Your secrets stay in your browser.

Password-derived keys

Keys are derived from your password using PBKDF2 with 100,000 iterations. Even if someone intercepts the encrypted URL, they can't decrypt it without your password. Brute-force attacks are computationally expensive by design.

Open source & auditable

View the source code. No obfuscation, no minification, no tricks. Every line of encryption code is visible. You don't have to trust us—you can verify the security yourself.
